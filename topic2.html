Users can access and store data and apps on distant server instead of local devices thanks to technology called cloud computing.This strategy makes computing resources, including storage, processing power, and applications, available online whenever needed. To suit their unique requirements, users can make use of a variety of cloud service models, such as Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS), and Software as a Service (SaaS). In IBM's article on Cloud Computing, they say Cloud Computing is used everyday and it also states "With cloud computing, you get the computing power or storage you need, without having to own or manage the physical hardware yourself."(Susnjara & Smalley, 2025). Because users may access resources from any location with an internet connection, cloud computing offers advantages like scalability, cost-effectiveness, and enhanced collaboration. This move to cloud-based solutions has changed how companies run, increasing the efficiency and accessibility of technology.

Pros and Cons

Cloud Computing offers many pros such as flexibility, cost savings, and scalability, allowing organizations to quickly adapt to changing demands. Nevertheless, there are some disadvantages to the cloud computing, despite how many benefits are included, such as concerns over data privacy, potential downtime, and reliance on third-party providers for security and service continuity. According to Liquid Web's article on the Advantages and Disadvantages of Cloud Computing, another disadvantage is Control because cloud computing services are managed by the service provider, meaning you won't have any responsibility for maintaining and updating the cloud infrastructure, giving you limited control over the infrastructure (Web, 2024).
