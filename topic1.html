Ethical hacking, AKA pen testing, involves authorized attempts to compromise computer system, networks, or applications to exploit weaknesses and improve security. In contrast to malevolent hackers, ethical hackers work with authorization from the company they are testing with the goal of improving security protocols and protecting safeguarding private information. According to the National Cybersecurity Alliance's article on Ethical Hacking, it states,"ethical hackers are vital to protecting businesses from cyber threats" which means for every corporation, they should equip a skilled white hat hacker to stop cyber intrusions (National Cybersecurity Alliance, 2024).They simulate assaults, evaluate possible threats, and offer practical suggestions for bolstering defenses using a variety of tools and methods. In addition to assisting businesses in protecting their data, the practice promotes proactive risk management and security awareness.

Pros and Cons

Ethical hacking has various advantages like proactively identifying and mitigating security vulnerabilities before malicious hackers can exploit the. This practice enhances overall cybersecurity, builds trust with clients, and helps organizations comply with regulatory standards. According to GeeksforGeeks article on Ethical hacking pros and cons, they state "Ethical Hacking helps to fight against cyber terrorism and to fight against national security breaches" which is a huge advantage for white hat hackers. However, there are some disadvantages to ethical hacking such as Corrupted files, increased costs to the company, hampers security operation, and there is a chance of data exposure.

Tools Purpose
